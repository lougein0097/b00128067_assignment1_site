The Beautiful City of Istanbul

Istanbul is a delightful city full of historical sites and kind, welcoming people. Its previous titles are Byzantium and Constantinople, which are filled with culture. The monumental, historical works of art within this city are immaculate, especially the antiquity from both empires, the Ottoman Empire and the Byzantine Empire, their impact still remains in the heart of Istanbul, this is what's most likely responsible for the city's constant visiting of tourists.

The Bosphorus River flows through the city's center. This river splits Istanbul since this river flows through the continental borders of Europe and Asia. The passage connecting the Black Sea and the Marmara Sea not only connects the two contents geographically together but spiritually too. This is the world's smallest passage.

European Side

The European Side contains Istanbul's historical base, which contains iconic attractions such as the lavish Topkapi Palace, the Blue Mosque, and Hagia Sophia, which was a former church but now its a mosque famous for its Byzantine mosaics. The vibrant Spice Market is located in the harbor side of Eminönü where in the Asian side of Istanbul can be seen in the distance and also the Grand Bazaar, one of Istanbul's most famous sights, where stalls sell carpets, fabrics, and jewels.  Stores, cafes, and restaurants are always filled with queues of people.

Asian Side

On the Asian side, there are coastal attractions such as the embellished Beylerbeyi Palace and the 15th-century Şemsi Paşa Mosque. On a small island, the phenomenal Maiden's Tower can be seen and admired, offering a panoramic view of the city and division between both continents. The Kadıköy district is most famous for its vibrant market and sculptures gracing the narrow streets The views from the Moda harbour stretches across the Sea of Marmara to the historic centre, which is the European side.
