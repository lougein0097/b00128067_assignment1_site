Tourism

Istanbul seems to create an appealing balance between the elegant design of its heritage places and the far more urban appearance of its skyscrapers and shopping malls. There are many interesting spots to explore in and around the area, as well as locations with stunning views of the city and the Marmara Sea.

You can admire majestic mosques, beautiful temples, fascinating museums, and Sultans' (Sovereign) palaces, or go on a walk to the Bosphorus to eat fresh fish and overlook the Black Sea.

Hagia Sophia

Hagia Sophia, a significant Byzantine architecture in Istanbul and one of the world's major standing monuments. It was constructed as a Christian church in the sixth century under the rule of the Byzantine emperor Justinian, it has since become a Mosque and a museum. The engravings of Islam, as well as the luxurious mosaics of Christianity, represent the religious changes over the centuries.

Blue Mosque

The Sultanahmet Mosque was built between 1609 and 1617 and is regarded as the Blue Mosque because of the blue tiles used to beautify its internal walls. Sultan Ahmet I funded the building of the Blue Mosque. The plan was to build a structure that would not only compete with, but also outperform, the glorious Hagia Sophia. Both Hagia Sophia and the Blue Mosque are located in centre of Sultan Ahmet where tourist can visit.

Basilica Cistern

It is Istanbul's largest remaining Byzantine cistern, built with 336 columns, many of which were reclaimed from destroyed temples.  Its elegance and utter magnificence of formation are stunning, and its depths provide a pleasant break on hot summer days.

Topkapi Palace

Topkapi Palace Museum houses the Ottoman Empire's royal artefacts as well as a significant archive of books and manuscripts in its library. It is famous for its four courtyards, which include the Audience Hall, the High Court, the Historical Kitchens, and The Treasury.

Grand Bazaar

The Grand Bazaar is often described as It is the heart, the brain and the spirit of Istanbul by its locals and many tourists would agree with this. Not only can you shop here but you can experience the history through engravings in the walls, ceilings, mosaics and paintings.

Turkish Baths

This is referred to as a "hammam" in Turkish. The public hammam is still an important part of the tradition, serving as a space for cleansing as well as relaxation. The oldest and most famous baths in Istanbul are Ayasofya Hurrem Sultan Bath, Cagaloglu, Cemberlitas, Suleymaniye, and Galatasaray Baths. Female and male sections are separated in traditional hammams.
